Crimping and Why it's Helpful

Crimping wigs is maybe the best piece of cosplay advice I've been given in the whole seven years I've been cosplaying, but what are the benefeits of crimping?

Crimping wigs gives amazing benefeits and its absolutely worth it. Crimping allows wigs to be malluable and easy to group together to shape because of the way that the crimper waves the fibers, which also thickens the wig so if it's a cheap and thin wig (which cosplay wigs usually are) it reduces the show of the wigcap and netting, which is super useful and convenient. Crimping wigs allows hte fibers to actually be hard to tangle, unlike uncrimped synthetic wigs. Wigs tend to have a shiny-plastic look to them and crimping makes the wigs more natural looking and less artificially shiny, but still keeps the likeness of the cosplayed character.

Crimping Tips For Success

  • Crimping thin sections one at a time, usually about half a centimeter in height
  • Brush weft before crimping to avoid any knots in the crimp
  • Brushing out the crimped section about five seconds after the weft is done being crimped to avoid obvious crimp marks
  • This goes along with any heat tools on a wig; turning the crimper down to the lowest heat setting
  • Start with the back section first, and then go onto bangs/front of wig to avoid tangling while styling front
  • If cutting off wefts to build a wig from scalp up, crimp BEFORE you cut wefts off
  • Dont hold crimper on sections of wefts for too long, usually about three seconds or less, depending on heat intensity and type of fiber
  • Try not to put products in the wig before crimping
